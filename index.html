Dennis Ritchie

Creator of the C programming language and
co-creator of the Unix operating system.

Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

Dennis Ritchie was born in Bronxville, New York. His father was Alistair E. Ritchie,
a longtime Bell Labs scientist and co-author of The Design of Switching Circuits
on switching circuit theory.

As a child, Dennis moved with his family to Summit, New Jersey, where he graduated
from Summit High School.He graduated from Harvard University with degrees in physics
and applied mathematics in 1963.

In 1967, Ritchie began working at the Bell Labs Computing Sciences Research Center.
In 1968, he defended his PhD thesis on "Computational Complexity and Program Structure"
at Harvard under the supervision of Patrick C. Fischer. However, Ritchie never officially
received his PhD degree as he did not submit a bound copy of his dissertation to the Harvard library,
a requirement for the degree.
In 2020, the Computer History Museum worked with Ritchie's family and Fischer's family and found
a copy of the lost dissertation.
